## Project Background 
Sales Performance Analytics System is a data analysis project that uses historical sales data to support informed business decision-making. It explores sales transactions, customers, products, locations, and order status to identify revenue trends, customer behavior patterns, product performance, and regional sales distribution. The project helps uncover key insights such as performance gaps, top-performing segments, and growth opportunities. The findings are presented through interactive Power BI reports to simplify analysis and support effective decision-making.
--- 
 
## Project Objective 
i.	To analyze historical sales data in order to understand overall business performance and how it has changed over time.
ii.	To track and compare sales trends across different time periods including daily, monthly, quarterly, and yearly performance for better trend analysis.
iii.	To evaluate sales performance across various geographic levels such as countries, cities, states, and territories to identify strong and weak markets.
iv.	To assess product line performance in order to identify top-selling products and areas where sales are declining.
v.	To track and analyze order status including shipped, cancelled, on hold, resolved, in process, and disputed to evaluate operational performance.
vi.	To identify the customer with the highest number of orders in order to understand key customer engagement and loyalty patterns
vii.	To support data-driven decision-making by presenting insights in a clear, structured, and interactive Power BI reporting format.
--- 
 
## Datasets 

**Sales.csv ** -  This dataset contains information about sellers on the platform. It includes the following columns: seller ID, seller zip code prefix, seller city, and seller state. Each record represents an individual seller and their location details.


--- 
## Dashboard Features 
Key KPIs: total quantity ordered, average price per unit (price each), and total sales revenue. These KPIs provide a clear summary of overall sales performance, pricing trends, and demand levels across different segments.
Visuals include: line charts for trends over years, pie charts, clustered column and bar charts  and tables for detailed records and contact information.
Interactivity: slicers for Year ID, Customer Name, and Product Line allow users to filter and explore the data dynamically.

## Key Findings
•	Diego Freyre stands out as the top customer, contributing the highest sales in the dataset, showing strong repeat purchases and making him a key driver of revenue for the business. 
•	Most orders are marked as “Shipped”, indicating that the majority of sales are successfully completed and delivered, which reflects an efficient order fulfillment process. 
•	Sales are highest on Day 5, peak in November (Month 11), and are strongest in 2004, showing clear patterns in daily, monthly, and yearly performance. 
•	The city of Madrid records the highest sales, the state of California (CA) ranks second in sales performance, the USA is the leading country in revenue contribution, and the EMEA territory is the strongest performing region overall. 
•	Quarter 4 records the highest sales while Quarter 3 has the lowest, showing that sales generally peak towards the end of the year after a mid-year slowdown. 
•	The Classic Cars product line generates the highest revenue, followed by Vintage Cars, showing that a few strong product categories drive most of the business income. 
•	The Trains product line performs the weakest, indicating low demand compared to other categories and suggesting it may need further review. 
•	A small group of customers contributes a large share of total revenue, showing that the business is highly dependent on key customers for performance. 
•	Sales fluctuate over time, showing clear seasonal patterns that indicate customer demand changes depending on the time of year. 
•	There is a noticeable drop in sales in one of the years, which may indicate changes in market conditions, demand, or internal business challenges. 


--- 
## Recommendations 
•	The business should focus more on high-performing product lines like Classic Cars by strengthening marketing efforts and ensuring consistent availability to meet demand. 
•	Underperforming product categories such as Trains should be reviewed and improved through strategies like promotions, bundling, or repositioning to increase demand. 
•	Strong relationships should be maintained with top customers like Diego Freyre through loyalty programs and personalized engagement to encourage continued purchases. 
•	The customer base should be expanded to reduce over-reliance on a small group of high-value customers and ensure more stable revenue streams. 
•	Expansion into underperforming regions should be prioritized to balance geographic sales and reduce dependence on dominant markets like the USA. 
•	The cause of the sales decline in the affected year should be investigated to understand the issue and prevent similar occurrences in the future. 
•	The business should prepare for peak demand periods such as November and Quarter 4 by increasing stock levels and marketing efforts in advance. 
•	During slower periods like Quarter 3, targeted promotions and discounts should be used to boost sales and maintain consistent performance throughout the year.
